Bree Wasylenko – Professional Life

Professionally, Bree Wasylenko is a dancer. She gained immense success in the industry and she also competed in the Top 20 of So You Think You Can Dance Canada season 3. Bree is also an actor with her involvement as the character Kate in the Family Channel TV show The Next Step.

Bree is also a choreographer as well as an instructor. Before her fame, Bree trained in numerous styles of dance growing up and also worked as an assistant on So You Think You Can Dance Canada in seasons 1, 2, and 4. Moreover, she also performed as a dancer on the CW’s Nikita. Then she was a dancer in Canada’s Got Talent and Over the Rainbow. Bree also features on The Next Step alongside Alexandra Beaton.

Likewise, Bree has appeared in music videos of popular singers as well. More precisely, she has performed for Carly Rae Jepsen and Shawn Desman among others. She was an assistant choreographer for Frenemies, a 2012 teen comedy-drama, and also in the TV show American Royalty. Bree then featured Baxter. 

As a choreographer, Bree has also worked on commercials for brands like Pepperidge Farms for the Feel the Crunch commercial and Staples for the Back to School commercial. Bree worked on the 2011 TV movie Stay with Me as the lead choreographer.

More Details

Bree made her acting debut with the drama TV movie Alive released on 5 February 2013. For this movie, her role was the character of Roxy. She also played the role of Clarissa Eldridge in a 2015 episode of the CBC series Murdoch Mysteries. Clarissa is a wealthy housewife of the early 1900s. Likewise, she is also a laundress in the CW series Reign.

Then in 2017, Bree appeared as Kiki Razzlein in an episode of the Canadian news/satire/comedy series The Beaverton. Then she played the role of Shava in a 2018 episode of the web series Star Trek: Discovery. Some other TV projects include the Canadian children’s comedy series, Baxter and Nikita. She also starred as a dancer in the series Over the Rainbow. Bree made her film debut as Venus in the 2018 science-fiction crime drama Kin.

Bree Wasylenko has also been part of the dance crew of several live stage shows. She was a part of the live shows of Howie D, Keisha Chante, and Danny Fernandes. She was also part of the 2011 Parapan Games and the drama Iphigenia in Tauris and appeared in RAIN – Life in Full Bloom and Verb. Atum. Both of these were the creations of choreographer Linda Garneau.

Likewise, some music videos that Bree has choreographed are Sam Smith’s That Much Closer and Shad’s Remember 2 Remember. Along with choreographer Caroline Torti, she also served as the co-director of Collective Elite Entertainment. Bree is also the choreographer of events for various top-notch brands like Calvin Klein, CIBC, TIFF, and the City of Toronto’s Cavalcade of Lights.

Bree is also the choreographer of Carly Rae Jepsen’s performance at the 2012 MMVAs. She is also the co-director of a short film named Ruin and it is produced under Collective Elite Entertainment. Bree, along with the cast of The Next Step then created the Pure Dance Convention. It is a full-day dance workshop that runs across Canada. It is primarily for budding dancers and focuses mainly on giving them an intensive workshop experience.
