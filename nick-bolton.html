Early Life & Family

Nicholas Bolton, popularly known as Nick Bolton, was born on March 10, 2000, in Frisco, Texas, United States. He is the eldest son of Carlos and Jalunda Bolton, originally from Texas.

He was brought up with his siblings in his homeland, Frisco. Bolton has a half-sister named Jazmin Dunn. In addition, he has three younger siblings.

Nick enrolled in a local school nearby his residence in Frisco. Although he was not the top student in his class, the young boy showed exceptional interest in extracurricular activities.

His muscular physique, incredible athleticism, and interest in sports from a young age helped him become the player he is today. Bolton has been drawn to football since he was a toddler.

His father, Carlos, was a huge football fan, so he made all his children watch the games together with him on TV whenever he had free time.

Nick often re-enacted the moves he saw on the television and practiced them with his siblings in their living room.

As his elder half-sister, Jazmin, was an outstanding football player herself, it was easy for Bolton to have regular football practice sessions at home.

Sadly, his elder sister could not continue her sports career as she suffered a severe illness later.

On the brighter side, however, Jazmin always stood by Nick and watched him become a better linebacker as the days went by. Nick completed his high school education at Lone Star High School in his hometown.

After graduation, he joined the University of Missouri for his higher education. However, he left his academics unfinished to pursue a professional football career.

College Career At Missouri

After having an excellent high school career at Lone Star, Nick opted to play college football for the Missouri Tigers in 2018. While with the Lone Star, he worked harder on improving his skills and recorded 130 tackles in his senior season.

However, Nick’s first season at Missouri did not go as expected, as he could only achieve 22 tackles in all 13 games.

Fortunately, he made a remarkable comeback the following season, recording 103 tackles.

Although his junior season was cut short and reduced to just ten games because of Covid-19, he had 95 tackles and two sacks in 2020.

The player received many honors for his performance, including First-team All-SEC and AP Second-team All-American.

NFL Career

Almost as predicted, Kansas City Chiefs drafted Nick Bolton as the 58th overall pick in the 2021 NFL Draft. He eventually signed a deal with the Chiefs on May 13, 2021. 

Bolton played 16 matches in his rookie season, finishing it with 112 tackles, one fumble recovery, and one defensive touchdown.

Following his rookie season performance, the PFF’s Sam Monson ranked him 16th Best NFL Linebacker.

Furthermore, Nick also won the Mac Lee Hill Rookie of the Year for his performance in the 2021-22 season.

Bolton finished the 2022 regular season with 180 tackles – the second-most in the league behind Jaguars’ Foye Oluokun (184 tackles) and a single-season franchise record of the Kansas City Chiefs.
